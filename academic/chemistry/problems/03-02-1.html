Question ​

2 N2O5(g)→4NO2(g)+O2(g)2 \mathrm{~N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5(g) \rightarrow 4 \mathrm{NO}_2(g)+\mathrm{O}_2(g) 2 N2​O5​(g)→4NO2​(g)+O2​(g)

The decomposition of N2O5(g)\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5(g)N2​O5​(g) is represented by the equation above. A sample of N2O5(g)\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5(g)N2​O5​(g) is monitored as it decomposes, and the concentration of N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​ as a function of time is recorded. The results are shown in the table below.

Time (s)[N2O5]\mathrm{[N_2O_5]}[N2​O5​]
01.000

50.00.642

Calculate the average rate of the reaction between 50.050.050.0 and 75.075.075.0 seconds.

Solution ​

a A+b B→cC+dDa \mathrm{~A}+b \mathrm{~B} \rightarrow c \mathrm{C}+d \mathrm{D} a A+b B→cC+dD

the rate of reaction is defined as

 rate =−1aΔ[A]Δt=−1bΔ[B]Δt=+1cΔ[C]Δt=+1dΔ[D]Δt\color{cyan}\text { rate }=-\frac{1}{a} \frac{\Delta[\mathrm{A}]}{\Delta t}=-\frac{1}{b} \frac{\Delta[\mathrm{B}]}{\Delta t}=+\frac{1}{c} \frac{\Delta[\mathrm{C}]}{\Delta t}=+\frac{1}{d} \frac{\Delta[\mathrm{D}]}{\Delta t}  rate =−a1​ΔtΔ[A]​=−b1​ΔtΔ[B]​=+c1​ΔtΔ[C]​=+d1​ΔtΔ[D]​

Notice that the rate of change in concentration of each species is divided by its coefficient from the balanced chemical equation ( aaa, b,cb, cb,c, or ddd ). This ensures that the calculated reaction rate is the same no matter which reactant or product is monitored for changes in concentration. In this case, the monitored species was N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​. With that in mind, let's write the reaction rate in terms of the rate of change in concentration of N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​ :

 rate =−12Δ[N2O5]Δt\text { rate }=-\frac{1}{2} \frac{\Delta\left[\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5\right]}{\Delta t}  rate =−21​ΔtΔ[N2​O5​]​

Since the coefficient for N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​ in the balanced equation is 2 , we divided the rate of change in concentration of N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​ by 2 . Additionally, since N2O5\mathrm{N}_2 \mathrm{O}_5N2​O5​ is being consumed in the reaction, we included a negative sign in front of the expression.

Now, let's plug in the information from the table to calculate the average reaction rate between 50.050.050.0 and 75.075.075.0 seconds:

 rate =−12(0.515M−0.642M)(75.0 s−50.0 s)=2.54×10−3M s−1\begin{aligned} \text { rate } & =-\frac{1}{2} \frac{(0.515 M-0.642 M)}{(75.0 \mathrm{~s}-50.0 \mathrm{~s})} \\ & =2.54 \times 10^{-3} M \mathrm{~s}^{-1} \end{aligned}  rate ​=−21​(75.0 s−50.0 s)(0.515M−0.642M)​=2.54×10−3M s−1​

So, the average rate of the reaction between 50.050.050.0 and 75.075.075.0 seconds is 2.54×10−3Ms−1\bold{2.54 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{M} \mathrm{s}^{-1}}2.54×10−3Ms−1.
